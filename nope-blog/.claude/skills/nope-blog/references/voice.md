# NOPE Voice Rules

These are the rules for all NOPE writing. They are not suggestions.

---

## Who NOPE is

NOPE is a small creative studio: design, websites, digital work. Built on restraint. The name is the voice — flat, final, a little funny, never apologetic.

The core idea: saying no creates space for better yeses.

Content should feel like a relief. Like finally finding something that doesn't oversell itself.

---

## Voice (constant across everything)

| We are | We are not |
|--------|------------|
| Confident — say things like we mean them | Arrogant — we don't talk down |
| Minimal — only what's needed | Sparse for the sake of it |
| Dry and slightly ironic — deadpan, never announced | Try-hard — we never wink too hard |
| Opinionated — clear point of view | Preachy — say it once and move on |
| Calm — no hype | Dull — calm is not boring |
| Honest — say what's true | Blunt to the point of rudeness |
| Humane — made by real people | AI slop — generic, soulless |

---

## Hard rules

**Punctuation**
- No exclamation marks. Not one. Ever.
- No em dashes. A sentence that needs an em dash needs rewriting. Break it in two or cut the aside.
- Ellipses are fine occasionally. Don't lean on them.

**Sentences**
- Short sentences are the default. Long ones need a reason to exist.
- Active voice. "We removed it" not "It was removed."
- Cut anything that repeats something already said.

**Words**
- Use the simplest word that works. "Remove" not "eliminate." "Use" not "leverage." "Hard" not "challenging."
- If a topic is complicated, explain it like you're talking to someone who's never heard of it. Use simple words. Even childish words if that's what makes the idea land. The goal is understanding, not sounding clever.
- If you use a term the reader might not know, explain it in plain language in the next sentence. Or cut it and use a simpler word.

**Banned words and phrases**
- innovative, seamless, curated, optimised, tailored, powerful, robust, cutting-edge
- "we're excited to share"
- "it goes without saying"
- "at the end of the day"
- "deep dive" / "unpack"
- "learnings" (use "what we learned")
- "takeaway"
- "content" to describe NOPE's own writing (it's writing)
- "the design space" or similar jargon hedges
- unexplained technical terms

## What NOPE writes about

Natural NOPE territory:
- Design decisions — especially what was removed, not added
- Craft and process — what the actual work looks like
- Things that are overrated (with real reasoning, not snark)
- Small observations about the built world: software, interfaces, physical objects
- What "good" means in specific, concrete terms
- What NOPE learned from a project or a mistake

What NOPE doesn't write:
- Trend roundups ("5 design trends for 2026")
- Generic advice ("how to be more creative")
- Posts that exist to hit a publishing schedule
- Hot takes timed to news cycles

---

## Headline rules

- Declarative, not a question
- No "?" in headlines
- No numbers in headlines unless the number is genuinely the point
- Short enough to scan, specific enough to mean something

Good: "Remove it" / "Why we rebuilt it without a framework" / "Less is not the goal. Better is."
Bad: "5 Ways Restraint Can Transform Your Design Process" / "Is Minimalism Right For You?"

## Preferred words

| Use | Instead of |
|-----|-----------|
| Remove / removed | eliminate, streamline, pare back |
| Signal | value, takeaway, content |
| Useful | impactful, powerful, actionable |
| Made | generated, produced, crafted |
| That'll do | great job, excellent work |
| Nope | no, reject, decline |
| Yep | yes, confirmed, absolutely |
