kind: note
title: Evals are the unit tests you keep skipping
date: 2026-04-09
tags: [llms, engineering]
---

Every team building on top of LLMs says they'll write evals later. Later never comes until something breaks in production and the fix breaks three other things.

An eval doesn't have to be fancy — even a handful of representative inputs with expected outputs catches regressions when you swap models or tweak prompts. The discipline is the same as testing: the cost of skipping it is always higher than it looks upfront.
